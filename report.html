Velocity Equation

Description:

This equation defines the velocity \( \vec{v}(t) \) as the time derivative of the position vector \( \vec{r}(t) \).

Original Format:

\[{\vec{v}}(t)={\frac{d{\vec{r}}(t)}{d t}}.\]

Symbols:

Symbol Description
\(\vec{v}(t)\) Velocity vector at time t
\(\vec{r}(t)\) Position vector at time t
\(t\) Time variable

Speed from Velocity Magnitude

Description:

This equation defines speed as the magnitude of the velocity vector.

Original Format:

\[v=|\vec{v}(t)|\]

Symbols:

Symbol Description
\(v\) Speed
\(\vec{v}(t)\) Velocity vector at time t

Acceleration Equation

Description:

This equation defines acceleration \( \vec{a}(t) \) as the time derivative of velocity \( \vec{v}(t) \) and the second time derivative of position \( \vec{r}(t) \).

Original Format:

\[\vec{a}(t)=\frac{d\vec{v}(t)}{d t}=\frac{d^{2}\vec{r}(t)}{d t^{2}}.\]

Symbols:

Symbol Description
\(\vec{a}(t)\) Acceleration vector as a function of time
\(\vec{v}(t)\) Velocity vector at time t
\(\vec{r}(t)\) Position vector at time t

Newton's Second Law

Description:

Newton’s Second Law states that the net force \( \vec{F} \) acting on a particle of mass \( m \) produces an acceleration \( \vec{a} \).

Original Format:

\[{\vec{F}}=m{\vec{a}}.\]

Symbols:

Symbol Description
\(\vec{F}\) Net force acting on a particle
\(m\) Mass of the particle
\(\vec{a}\) Acceleration of the particle

Newton's Second Law in terms of Momentum

Description:

This equation expresses Newton's Second Law of Motion in terms of the time rate of change of momentum. It states that the force acting on a particle is equal to the derivative of its momentum with respect to time.

Original Format:

\[{\vec{F}}={\frac{d{\vec{p}}}{d t}},\quad{\mathrm{where~}}{\vec{p}}=m{\vec{v}}.\]

Symbols:

Symbol Description
\(\vec{F}\) Net force acting on a particle
\(\vec{p}\) Linear momentum of the particle
\(t\) Time variable
\(m\) Mass of the particle
\(\vec{v}\) Velocity of the particle

Infinitesimal Work Equation

Description:

This equation represents the infinitesimal work done by a force \( \vec{F} \) acting on a particle undergoing a displacement \( \vec{d r} \).

Original Format:

\[d W={\vec{F}}\cdot{\vec{d r}}.\]

Symbols:

Symbol Description
\(d W\) Infinitesimal work done
\(\vec{F}\) Net force acting on a particle
\(\vec{d r}\) Infinitesimal displacement vector

Work Equation

Description:

This equation represents the total work done by a force \( \vec{F} \) acting on a particle along a path, calculated by integrating the dot product of the force and the differential displacement \( d\vec{r} \).

Original Format:

\[W=\int{\vec{F}}\cdot d{\vec{r}}.\]

Symbols:

Symbol Description
\(W\) Work done
\(\vec{F}\) Net force acting on a particle
\(d\vec{r}\) Infinitesimal displacement vector

Work-Energy Theorem

Description:

This equation relates the work done (W) on a particle to the change in its kinetic energy (ΔK).

Original Format:

\[W=\Delta K\]

Symbols:

Symbol Description
\(W\) Work done
\(\Delta K\) Change in kinetic energy

Moment of Inertia Equation

Description:

This equation defines the moment of inertia (I) as the sum of the products of the mass (m_i) of each particle and the square of its distance (r_i) from the axis of rotation.

Original Format:

\[I=\sum_{i}m_{i}r_{i}^{2}\]

Symbols:

Symbol Description
\(I\) Moment of inertia
\(m_{i}\) Mass of the i-th particle
\(r_{i}\) Distance of the i-th particle from the axis of rotation

Symbol Description

Simple Harmonic Motion Equation

Description:

This equation describes the motion of a simple harmonic oscillator, such as a mass on a spring, where the acceleration is proportional to the displacement and directed towards the equilibrium position.

Original Format:

\[\frac{d^{2}x}{d t^{2}}+\frac{k}{m}x=0.\]

Symbols:

Symbol Description
\(\frac{d^{2}x}{d t^{2}}\) Second derivative of displacement with respect to time (acceleration)
\(x\) Displacement from equilibrium
\(t\) Time variable
\(k\) Spring constant
\(m\) Mass of the particle

Symbols:

Symbol Description
\(x(t)\) Position of the oscillator at time t
\(A\) Amplitude of the oscillation
\(\omega\) Angular velocity
\(t\) Time variable
\(\phi\) Phase constant
